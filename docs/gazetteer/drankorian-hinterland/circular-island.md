# The Circular Island
<div class="grid cards ext-narrow-margin ext-one-column" markdown>
-    :octicons-location-24:{ .lg .middle } An island in the [Sea of Storms](<sea-of-storms.md>)  
</div>




The Circular Island, so-called for is unique perfectly circular shape, is a remote island in the [Sea of Storms](<sea-of-storms.md>), off the coast of the [Drankorian Hinterland](<drankorian-hinterland.md>). The island is difficult to approach, ringed by violent storms and protected by old magic that interferes with ordinary travel and divination, and the old reliable modern accounts of it come from the travels of the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>). 

The island contains many ancient ruins, dating to the days of the [Drankorian Empire](<../../history/historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>) or even before, but the exact nature of these ruins, what once occupied the island, and its former importance is now mostly forgotten. 



For those who have visited and explored the Circular Island, and uncovered some of its secrets, the story is much richer. Hidden by storms and guarded against magic, the Circular Island preserves something of Drankorian culture. It contains many ruins, ancient [Mos Numena](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/mos-numena/mos-numena.md>) pilgrimage sites, [Apollyon's Tower](<apollyon-s-tower.md>), and hints of an ancient connection to [Hkar](<../../history/historical-realms/hkar.md>). 

However, the Circular Island is near-impossible to reach. It is protected by a massive, permanent storm that dispels all magic passing through it, and it is guarded against all magical scrying and teleportation. The only practical way to enter is to [brave the storm and sail](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-106-dufr.md>). 

### History

The oldest traces on the island tell of tales that date to the days of the destruction of [Hkar](<../../history/historical-realms/hkar.md>) and the [Downfall Wars](<../../events/ancient/downfall-wars.md>). Reliefs and prayers carved on pilgrimage sites show the island itself somehow walking through the sea, guided by priests, wizards, and the [Eight Divines](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/incorporeal-gods/mos-numena-pantheon/mos-numena-pantheon.md>), carrying refugees fleeing the destruction of [Hkar](<../../history/historical-realms/hkar.md>) and The Downfall to safety. Once it settled in place off the coast of [Drankor](<../../history/historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>), it became a major pilgrimage site: memorials of [Hkar](<../../history/historical-realms/hkar.md>), the [Downfall Wars](<../../events/ancient/downfall-wars.md>), and those who died in that tragedy dot the island, including a major memorial to fallen elves. 

In later Drankorian centuries, the island also became a place of magical study. Scholarly societies established villas, towers, libraries, and research communities among the older sacred sites. The [Concordia Pyrae](<../../groups/drankorian-societies/concordia-pyrae.md>) kept records there, including research into the [Elemental Plane of Fire](<../../cosmology/energy-realms/elemental-plane-of-fire.md>) and chronicles of their villa's history; many other unexplored ruins likely hold many more secrets of other Drankorian societies. 

In the final decades of the [Drankorian Empire](<../../history/historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>), in the time leading up to and during [Apollyon](<../../people/historical-figures/drankorian-emperors/apollyon.md>)'s rule, the island was changed and consumed by the conflict between [Apollyon](<../../people/historical-figures/drankorian-emperors/apollyon.md>) and [Cha'mutte](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/cha-mutte.md>). Early in his reign, Apollyon established [a stronghold](<apollyon-s-tower.md>) on the far shore of the island, where he explored dark magic and eventually constructed the [dagger](<../../things/artifacts-of-power/apollyon-s-phylactery.md>) he used as a phylactery, and other materials required for lichdom. When conflict erupted between Apollyon and Cha'mutte, the main port and settlement was destroyed by the [First Plague](<../../events/1000s/1059/first-plague.md>), after which one of [Cha'mutte](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/cha-mutte.md>)'s spawn, [Ra'ghemdros](<../../people/other-nonhumans/ra-ghemdros.md>), made her lair on the island, guarding the imprisoned [Cloudspinner](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/archfey/cloudspinner.md>). 

### Locations

The island's coast is diverse, with stretches of sheer black cliffs interspersed with singing sand beaches and river mouths. Most notably, eight colossal statues of the Eight Divines stand around the coast, one for each of the divinities of [Mos Numena](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/mos-numena/mos-numena.md>). Broad white pilgrimage roads lead inland from them, toward the central mountain, still carrying ancient echos of powerful divine magic. Old camps and hostels along these routes preserve practical magic, including runic circles that provide water, food, and warmth, as well as hidden prayers in Drankorian and Elvish.

North of the statue of [The Wanderer](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/incorporeal-gods/mos-numena-pantheon/the-wanderer.md>) lies a ruined town and port, once the jewel of the island, but now destroyed and corrupted by plague curses. A number of collapsed towers, elegant manors, and the remnants of a pier still can be found, but patches of cursed land makes exploration for the unwary difficult. By 1749 DR, many of these ruins were occupied by Dragonets, occupying ruined manors and towers as far from plague-cursed lands as possible. 

Inland from the town are numerous villas of ancient scholary and magical societies, many of which have fallen into extreme disrepair. Among the better preserved is the villa of the Concordia Pyrae, which was taken over by [Ra'ghemdros](<../../people/other-nonhumans/ra-ghemdros.md>) for use as her lair, and adapted to serve as [Cloudspinner](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/archfey/cloudspinner.md>)'s prison. Cloudspinner's prison stood beneath its ruins, among the cellars, statuary, and magical flames of the old crypt, marked by a flaming red eye in shadow. Cloudspinner herself was in a suspended gray crystal, bound by shadow in a demi-plane and guarded by a fiend of flame and darkness.

The central interior of the island is forested, with a large mountain peak rising from the precise center of the island. The forested interior likely contains many secrets; it also preserves memorials to the [Downfall Wars](<../../events/ancient/downfall-wars.md>), such as a living elven memorial records names of the dead of the Downfall Wars, revealing memory-visions to those who are attuned to its message. However, not all of the pilgrimage magic has survived the ravages of plague and time inatact: some parts of the forest contain dream-haunted or memory-haunted sites, associated with visions of red mist and choking death.

At the summit of the central mountain stand a temple complex dedicated to the Eight Divines, with eight temples arranged around a central courtyard, beacon, bell, hostels, and pilgrim facilities. Lingering divine magic protects the complex, making it one of the island's few reliable sanctuaries in the modern era. 

The dragonets rarely travel to the western edge of the island, where [Apollyon's Tower](<apollyon-s-tower.md>) stands on a rugged coastal cliff as a black marble spire. It contains defensive constructs, decayed living spaces, a throne room, libraries, a magical forge of great power, and ritual chambers tied to Apollyon's lichcraft. Notes recovered there helped identify the nature of [Apollyon's Phylactery](<../../things/artifacts-of-power/apollyon-s-phylactery.md>) and the sacrifices used to create it.

### Inhabitants

The island's most powerful recent ruler was [Ra'ghemdros](<../../people/other-nonhumans/ra-ghemdros.md>), a black dragon and daughter of [Cha'mutte](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/cha-mutte.md>). She ruled from an acidic, shadow-tainted scar near the former villa of the [Concordia Pyrae](<../../groups/drankorian-societies/concordia-pyrae.md>), until her defeat at the hands of the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>). 

The bulk of the population of the Circular Island are dragonets. Small, dragon-like creatures of keen mind and mischievous spirit, they toiled under Ra'ghemdros for many years, surviving as best they could. While some dragonets were true believers in Ra'ghemdros's rule, many others were merely surviving under her authority, or looking for what advantage they could find. 

[Dimitaur](<../../people/pcs/great-war/companions/dimitaur.md>), an ancient dragonet and former companion of [Rai](<../../people/pcs/great-war/rai.md>), lived hidden in the summit temple complex. He remembered Rai, knew more than most dragonets about the island's deeper history, and opposed Ra'ghemdros's hold over the island. Since [Ra'ghemdros](<../../people/other-nonhumans/ra-ghemdros.md>)'s defeat, he has taken on a more prominent role in the island, though often with a focus primarily on ensuring he has more than enough to eat. 

The [Cloudspinner](<../../people/extraplanar-powers/archfey/cloudspinner.md>), also known as the Queen of Sunset, was imprisoned on the island for centuries, but was freed by the Dunmar Fellowship and eventually returned to [Amberglow](<../extraplanar/feywild/amberglow/amberglow.md>). 

### Recent Events

In May 1749 DR, the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>) [journeyed to the Circular Island](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-106-dufr.md>) in search of the final [Jade Piece of Rai's Hand](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/treasure/jade-piece-of-rai-s-hand.md>). They [explored the island](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-107-dufr.md>), [met dragonets](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-108-dufr.md>), and finally [acquired the jade from Ra'ghemdros](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-110-dufr.md>) by trading her Apollyon's throne, which they [recovered from his western tower](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-109-dufr.md>). They then [entered the Concordia Pyrae villa](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-110-dufr.md>), defeated the fiend guarding the shadow prison, and freed the Cloudspinner's crystal from its bindings. Ra'ghemdros [attacked the summit temple](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-110-dufr.md>) after Cloudspinner's release and was killed there. During and after the battle, factions of dragonets fought one another across the island. 

With Ra'ghemdros' death, [Dimitaur proclaimed himself Protector of the Forever Sunset](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-110-dufr.md>) and took charge of distributing or guarding what remained of Ra'ghemdros's hoard. But the present condition of the island remains unsettled, while the dragonets figure out how to make their own way.
