The Priest Thuhr is one of the seven traditional [dwarven](<../../creatures/species/dwarves.md>) *thuhr*: institutions of kinship and obligation that bind dwarven society together across clan and kingdom. In dwarven tradition, the Priest Thuhr is associated with [Fanrukel](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/embodied-gods/bahrazel/fanrukel.md>), the Priest among the [Bahrâzel](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/embodied-gods/bahrazel/bahrazel.md>).

## Thuhr in Dwarven Life

In the rite of name-taking at the [Heart of the Mountain](<../../cosmology/spiritual-realms/heart-of-the-mountain.md>), an adult dwarf receives both a name and a thuhr. The thuhr are a combination of guild, mentorship, secret society, and found family, and a dwarf’s ties to thuhr may be as central—or as incidental—as their ties to clan.
