# The Zimka

The Zimka are a highland people of the foothills of the [Sentinels](<../../gazetteer/sentinel-range.md>), whose homeland is [Zimkova](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/zimkova/zimkova.md>). In the tales of their villages, the Zimka came to this land long ago in a migration across the mountains; and in the bogs and lakes of the upper [Volta](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/volta.md>) they found the spirits they call the [kestavo](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/kestavo.md>).

## Identity and history

In the modern day, “Zimka” is often used as a single name for two closely related cultural strands with a shared identity:

- the ~Old Zimka~, a more isolated highland tradition that preserves the oldest ways and the language of [Old Zimkovan](<../../background/languages.md#old-zimkovan>)
- a broader lowland culture shaped by long contact with river traders and, later, with the expansion of [Drankor](<../../gazetteer/drankorian-hinterland/drankor/drankor.md>) and [Sembara](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/sembara/sembara.md>); in some places this strand follows [Mos Numena](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/mos-numena/mos-numena.md>) as readily as the kestavo

In the river valleys around the [Western Gulf](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/western-gulf.md>), old stories also remember the Zimka as one of the cultures that emerged from the mingling of the westward-moving ~Mariners~ and the older [Northerners](<northerners.md>) migrants in the years before The Downfall.

## Language

The oldest Zimka traditions are tied to [Old Zimkovan](<../../background/languages.md#old-zimkovan>), a Northros tongue. In most of the highland kingdoms of [Breva](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/zimkova/breva.md>) and [Ardlas](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/ardlas.md>), however, the everyday speech is usually [Zimkovan](<../../background/languages.md#zimkovan>), an Eastros-derived language that carries loan words and echoes of the older tongue.

## Religion

Many Zimka communities follow the [small-gods](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/northern-folk-religions.md>) tradition in the form of the [Kestavo](<../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/kestavo.md>). In Zimka rites, the kestavo are guides and messengers who carry prayers to the distant creator, and they are central to the customs of burial and remembrance; Zimka funerals are shaped around drawing the attention of a kestavo so that the dead are not lost on the perilous roads of the [Land of the Dead](<../../cosmology/land-of-the-dead.md>).

Zimka lore also warns of malevolent spirits such as [Zeyfa](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/kestavo/zeyfa.md>), the cruel west wind, and [Vaqar](<../../gods-and-religions/gods/kestavo/vaqar.md>), the fire-below—names spoken in the same breath as winter hardship, mischief, and sudden ruin.
