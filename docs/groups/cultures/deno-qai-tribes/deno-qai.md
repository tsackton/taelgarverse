# The Deno'qai

The Deno'qai are a branch of the [Northerner](<../northerners.md>) peoples, defined primarily by a shared religious and cultural tradition, especially the worship of the [tanshi](<../../../gods-and-religions/gods/tanshi/tanshi.md>), the small gods who carry prayers to [Anida](<../../../gods-and-religions/gods/high-gods/divine-presence.md>). They speak, Deno'qai, a group of related [Northros](<../../../background/languages.md#northros-language-family>) dialects and languages, and are organized into many tribes and villages. Known Deno'qai groups include the [Baz'aku](<elderwood-tribes/baz-aku.md>), [Bek'eni](<elderwood-tribes/bek-eni.md>), [Te'kula](<elderwood-tribes/te-kula.md>), [Ko'zula](<northern-tribes/ko-zula.md>), and [Shu'anra](<northern-tribes/shu-anra.md>).

Deno'qai tradition traces their ancestry to the earliest human migrations from [Hkar](<../../../history/historical-realms/hkar.md>), long before The Downfall and the founding of [Drankor](<../../../gazetteer/drankorian-hinterland/drankor/drankor.md>). Older Deno'qai stories describe a much wider range across the western forests, from the mountains to the sea and from the [Yeraad](<../../../gazetteer/greater-chardon/rivers/yeraad.md>) northward. Indeed, before the [Great War](<../../../events/1500s/great-war.md>), Deno'qai communities occupied much of the western slope of the Sentinels not claimed by elves, sometimes living in and among elven forests. However, the war and its aftermath devastated many Deno'qai communities, especially in the north, where those who survived the fighting retreated into high mountain redoubts. More recently, the slow recovery and reemergence of the Deno'qai in many of the surviving western forests has brought many tribes back into contact and sometimes conflict with the [Chardonian Empire](<../../../gazetteer/greater-chardon/chardonian-empire/chardonian-empire.md>), especially along the [Chasa River Valley](<../../../gazetteer/greater-chardon/chardonian-empire/chasa-river-valley/chasa-river-valley.md>). 

## Nomenclature

"Deno'qai" names both a people and a broader religious-cultural tradition. In [On the Lost People of the Forests](<../../../things/books/on-the-lost-people-of-the-forests.md>), the Chardonian scholar [Gnaeus](<../../../people/chardonians/gnaeus.md>) records a [Baz'aku](<elderwood-tribes/baz-aku.md>) explanation that Deno'qai means "People of the Gods" in their own tongue, but whether this is true is unknown. 

## Religion and Rituals
_See more: [Tanshi Worship](<../../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/tanshi-worship.md>)_

The primary Deno'qai religious tradition is [Tanshi Worship](<../../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/tanshi-worship.md>). The tanshi are minor divinities of the natural world, associated with forests, animals, rivers, stones, weather, hunting, and other small domains. The Deno'qai call on them to carry prayers to [Anida](<../../../gods-and-religions/gods/high-gods/divine-presence.md>), "the First and the Last," the Deno'qai name for the [Divine Presence](<../../../gods-and-religions/gods/high-gods/divine-presence.md>). 

Deno'qai ritual centers on offerings. A prayer must draw the attention of the appropriate tanshi, and each tanshi is approached through a sacrifice or offering suited to its nature. Many Deno'qai carry offering bags with small objects used in these rites. 

Deno'qai villages do not have temples: unlike [Mos Numena](<../../../gods-and-religions/religions/mos-numena/mos-numena.md>) or other pantheonic religions they do not see physical spaces as the locus of divinity. Nonetheless, they often do have ritual spaces, especially tanshi trees, stone altars, standing stones, or other natural features used for offerings. In many southern villages, the tanshi tree is an old, heavily tended tree near the center of the settlement.

Most Deno'qai villages and tribes have a Godcaller, a religious authority and lore-keeper responsible for the stories of the tanshi, the correct offerings, communal rites, and the memory of miracles. Some Godcallers wield divine magic, but many do not.

## Tribes

The Deno'qai are not a single polity. They are a patchwork of tribes, villages, and regional groups, linked by religion, language, shared origin traditions, and related customs. Village organization varies by region: while some tribes are organized around local chiefs and councils, others are more isolated or more strongly shaped by religious authority.

In the current day, most Deno'qai tribes occupy the forests and foothills between the Sentinels, to the west, and the edges of the Chardonian Empire, to the east, including the [Elderwood](<../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>), [Highveil Forest](<../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/highveil-forest.md>), and the [Northern Sentinels](<../../../gazetteer/northern-sentinels/northern-sentinels.md>). 

Among the Deno'qai of the [Elderwood](<../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>) are the [Baz'aku](<elderwood-tribes/baz-aku.md>), [Bek'eni](<elderwood-tribes/bek-eni.md>), and [Te'kula](<elderwood-tribes/te-kula.md>). The Baz'aku live on the edges of the forest and are known as traders and intermediaries with the [Chasa River Valley](<../../../gazetteer/greater-chardon/chardonian-empire/chasa-river-valley/chasa-river-valley.md>), while the Bek'eni and Te'kula live deeper in the Elderwood, where Deno'qai communities rely more heavily on hunting, trapping, forestry, and the shelter of the forest.

Farther north, the [Deno'qai communities of the Northern Sentinels](<northern-tribes/northern-tribes.md>) include the [Ko'zula](<northern-tribes/ko-zula.md>) and [Shu'anra](<northern-tribes/shu-anra.md>), isolated since the [Great War](<../../../events/1500s/great-war.md>). While they share a religion, language, and cultural history with other Deno'qai, they also practice distinct customs shaped by the high mountains, colder forests, hunting, and long contact with [Stoneborn](<../../../creatures/species/stoneborn.md>) and [Dwarves](<../../../creatures/species/dwarves.md>).
