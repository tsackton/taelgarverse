# The Bek'eni
:speaker:{ .middle } *(BEHK-en-ee)*  

 

The Bek'eni are one of the major [Deno'qai](<../deno-qai.md>) tribes based in the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>). Historically, they were largely hunters, foresters, and trappers, extensively trading and intermarrying with the [Baz'aku](<baz-aku.md>), but under the sway of [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) they became deeply isolationist and mistrusting. Since the unmasking of [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) as the green dragon [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>), and his death at the hands of the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>), the Bek'eni have struggled to recover. 

## Territory and Settlements 

The Bek'eni occupy numerous small villages and family hamlets scattered across the interior of the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>), with the densest settlements north and east of the [Kayan](<../../../../gazetteer/major-rivers/chasa-nahadi-watershed/kayan.md>). The core of Bek'eni territory is composed of fifteen or so small villages, most between fifty and one hundred people, stretching east along small, unmarked streams flowing into the [Kayan](<../../../../gazetteer/major-rivers/chasa-nahadi-watershed/kayan.md>). 

During the days of [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>)'s rule, the Bek'eni paid a high price in tribute, sending the bulk of their wealth and even a fair portion of their food to the dragon's hoard. Since Grimbaskal's death, some villages have recovered, but the Bek'eni remain poor, with few luxuries, problems with hunger, and dwellings in poor repair. 

## Local Culture 

The Bek'eni preserve a proud oral tradition that holds that their tribe long stood closest to the elves of [Ainumarya](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/ainumarya.md>), that in the ancient days they dwelt in friendship and commerce with the great elven cities of the forest. Their myths and tales speak of hidden elven ruins and old scars in the land where the forest does not return. These tales are old, predating [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>), and many are recorded by [Gnaeus](<../../../../people/chardonians/gnaeus.md>) in [On the Lost People of the Forests](<../../../../things/books/on-the-lost-people-of-the-forests.md>).

Under [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>)'s rule, the Bek'eni grew deeply isolationist and distrustful of outsiders. Bek'eni rumor would cast the [Baz'aku](<baz-aku.md>) as collaborators who have turned away from tradition, and the deep-forest [Te'kula](<te-kula.md>) as a people under a malign influence. 

Though many Bek'eni are still traditionalists with a deep appreciation for ancient culture, the upheavals of [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>)'s betrayal have caused much doubt and angst among some in the tribe.  

## Recent History

For decades, the Bek'eni followed the green dragon [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>), who posed as an elf under the name [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>). In 1748 DR, the elf called [Mezzar](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) was unmasked at a Deno'qai god tree as the green dragon [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) by the actions of the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>). Grimbaskal was slain soon after, and the corruption that had spread through the southern [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>) began to recede. The Bek'eni now reckon with the losses and divisions left behind by Mezzar's rule, including the disappearance of warriors taken into his service and the collapse of village authority built around him.
