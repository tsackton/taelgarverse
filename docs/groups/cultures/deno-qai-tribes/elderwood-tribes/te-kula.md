# The Te'kula

 

The Te'kula are a [Deno'qai](<../deno-qai.md>) tribe of the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>). For many years, they lived hidden in a forest demi-plane, protected from [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) by the tanshi [Aasimti](<../../../../gods-and-religions/gods/tanshi/aasimti.md>) and a [Jade Piece of Rai's Hand](<../../../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/treasure/jade-piece-of-rai-s-hand.md>). Since the [defeat of Grimbaskal](<../../../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/session-notes/session-52-dufr.md>) by the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>), the Te'kula have emerged from hiding and taken a leading role is restoring the forest ecosystem and the [Elderwood Tribes](<elderwood-tribes.md>) that suffered at [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>)'s hand. 
 
## Territory and Settlements

The Te'kula live deep in the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>), near the source of the [Kayan](<../../../../gazetteer/major-rivers/chasa-nahadi-watershed/kayan.md>). During their decades of magical isolation, they were a small tribe, consisting of only a single large village, surrounded by a few thousand homesteads and small family clusters. However, since [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>)'s death the Te'kula have grown significantly through immigration from other [Deno'qai](<../deno-qai.md>) [Elderwood Tribes](<elderwood-tribes.md>). 

## Local Culture

The Te'kula, along among the northern [Elderwood Tribes](<elderwood-tribes.md>), resisted and fought [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>), at great cost, leaving them isolated in magical demi-plane. This long history of isolation has colored the local culture of the tribe. 

Unlike most [Deno'qai](<../deno-qai.md>), they have a particularly close relationship with a single tanshi, [Aasimti](<../../../../gods-and-religions/gods/tanshi/aasimti.md>), who they call the spirit of the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>). Though the Te'kula do not deny the existence of other tanshi, most members of the tribe call on [Aasimti](<../../../../gods-and-religions/gods/tanshi/aasimti.md>) far more than is typical in [Tanshi Worship](<../../../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/tanshi-worship.md>). 

The Te'kula also preserve older Deno'qai traditions, maintained via years of isolation. 

## Recent history

For many years, the Te'kula lived quietly in the depths of the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>). This all changed in DR 1721, when [Jordo](<../../../../people/deno-qai/jordo.md>), the Godcaller of the [Te'kula](<te-kula.md>), found a fragment of [Rai's hand](<../../../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/treasure/jade-piece-of-rai-s-hand.md>) and united it with an ancient tree, a locus of Aasimit's power in the [Elderwood](<../../../../gazetteer/central-highlands/elderwood.md>). The spirit of [Rai](<../../../../people/pcs/great-war/rai.md>) and [Aasimti](<../../../../gods-and-religions/gods/tanshi/aasimti.md>) became entangled, with Rai providing a focus on the material plane to amplify and channel Aasimti's power. 

In DR 1722, when [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>) attacked the [Te'kula](<te-kula.md>) seeking the [Jade Piece of Rai's Hand](<../../../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/treasure/jade-piece-of-rai-s-hand.md>), Aasimti, channelling divine and arcane power through Rai, was able to shift the Te'kula village to a demiplane, protecting it from [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>). 

In DR 1748, the [Dunmar Fellowship](<../../../../people/pcs/dunmar-fellowship/dunmar-fellowship.md>) killed [Grimbaskal](<../../../../people/other-nonhumans/mezzar.md>), and were gifted the [Jade Piece of Rai's Hand](<../../../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/treasure/jade-piece-of-rai-s-hand.md>), disentangling Aasimti from [Rai](<../../../../people/pcs/great-war/rai.md>) and returning the Te'kula village to the material plane.
