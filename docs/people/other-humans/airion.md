Airion the Mistspeaker was a pre-[Great War](<../../events/1500s/great-war.md>) human elementalist and planar scholar, active mainly in the area that is now [Greater Voltara](<../../gazetteer/northwest-coast/northern-provinces/greater-voltara.md>) and the [Mawar Confederacy](<../../gazetteer/northwest-coast/mawar-confederacy/mawar-confederacy.md>). He is remembered chiefly for his deep study of the [Energy Realms](<../../cosmology/energy-realms/energy-realms.md>), though he also did significant work on elemental binding, and cataloged places where the boundary between the [Material Plane](<../../cosmology/material-plane.md>) and the elemental planes is thin. He died during the Great War, and much of his work was lost or scattered during the [Blood Years](<../../events/1500s/blood-years.md>).

His best-known surviving works are the [Elemental Scrolls of Airion](<../../things/artifacts-of-power/elemental-scrolls-of-airion.md>), four scrolls of elemental power that later drew the interest of the [Great Library](<../../gazetteer/greater-chardon/chardonian-empire/chardon/great-library.md>). The recovery of the scrolls by the [Silver Tempests](<../pcs/silver-tempests/silver-tempests.md>), along with some of his correspondence, and fragments such as [The Codex of Compound Planes](<../../things/books/the-codex-of-compound-planes.md>) restored enough of his scholarship to revive serious debate about compound elemental realms, planar weak points, and the structure of elemental magic.
