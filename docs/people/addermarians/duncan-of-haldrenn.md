Duncan of Haldrenn was a boastful shepherd from [Haldrenn](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/haldrenn.md>). He lived alone in a small stone cottage on the edge of [Haldrenn](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/haldrenn.md>), spending his summers with sheep in the high moors and winters around [Alewyn](<alewyn.md>)’s hearth, where villagers knew him as boastful and full of unlikely claims. During the winter of DR 1714-1715, his talk of hidden treasure became unusually serious, and in March 1715 he vanished into the [Highmoor](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/highmoor.md>) after claiming he knew where to find riches. 

His disappearance was later connected  to the anger of the [Briarheart](<../extraplanar-powers/archfey/morlaith.md>), and the investigation of the [Addermarch Mercenaries](<../pcs/addermarch/addermarch-mercenaries.md>) tied him to the [Serpentine Battleaxe](<../../things/magic-items/serpentine-battleaxe.md>), the stolen heart of the Singing Tree, and the ruins beneath [Serethwyn Tor](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/serethwyn-tor.md>). He was found dead beneath [Serethwyn Tor](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/serethwyn-tor.md>) on May 27, 1715 DR, along with a sheaf of letters to his associate [Kelvyn](<kelvyn.md>); the letters connected Duncan to [Geoffrey](<../other-humans/geoffrey.md>) and a mysterious patron searching for [serpentine](<../../things/materials/serpentine.md>).
