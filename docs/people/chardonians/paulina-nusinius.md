Paulina Nusinius was a Chardonian scholar and metaphysicist, who wrote shortly after the Great War on the relationship between Cha’mutte and Limbo. 

She is best known for the [provocative thesis](<../../campaigns/dunmari-frontier-campaign/letters-and-notes/research-about-limbo-and-cha-mutte.md>) that Cha’mutte was, in some sense, part of Limbo itself, and that at his death Limbo and the Material Plane briefly collapsed together. She argued that the post-war upheavals such as the rising of the [Yuvanti Mountains](<../../gazetteer/greater-dunmar/yuvanti-mountains.md>) and the collapse of [Urlich Pass](<../../gazetteer/central-highlands/urlich-pass.md>) could be interpreted as consequences of Limbo's temporary but powerful influence on the Material Plane.
