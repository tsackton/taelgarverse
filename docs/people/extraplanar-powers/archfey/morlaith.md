# Morlaith

Morlaith, also called the Briarheart, the Keeper of the Thornweald, and the Hidden Lord, is an ancient archfey who rules over a domain that lies congruent with parts of Addermarch, west of [Twilight's Grace](<../../../gazetteer/extraplanar/feywild/twilight-s-grace.md>) and east of Duskmire. He is old, strange, and remote: a faded remnant of the deep wild, an ancient echo of some forgotten dark forest of brambles, old paths, and hidden hollows. 

Morlaith's influence now lingers most strongly in the hills and river valleys of western [Addermarch](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/addermarch.md>), but old stories tell that his reach was once far greater, perhaps even stretching across much of what is now Maseau, Sembara, and Addermarch. His present domain is shrunken and uneven, with forgotten gates and crossings left behind where his attention has faded. This history is ancient, indistinct, and poorly recorded, more a matter of Addermarian folk legend than proper history. Standing stones, old warnings, and village rhymes preserve warnings of Morlaith, both the dangers of the careless and the songs and rituals to appease the forest. 

Morlaith does not maintain a court, or a manor, and does not engage with the politics of the Feywild. He is of the forest itself, dangerous unless appeased, but dangerous in the way the uncharted wilds are dangerous: not malevolent, but uncaring and perhaps even not fully aware of the small mortal things passing through his lands. His realms are not evil in any simple sense, but nor are they safe. Travelers who keep the old courtesies may pass where the careless are taken, changed, or lost.

## Addermarch Folklore

In [Addermarch](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/addermarch.md>), especially in the [Highmoor](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/highmoor.md>) and the older hill villages of the west, Morlaith is feared and placated rather than worshiped. Villagers set wards on ridgelines, leave small offerings, and repeat warnings and tales, out of respect for the ancient ways that kept their ancestors safe from the strangeness of the [Fey](<../../../creatures/fey/fey.md>). 

Around [Haldrenn](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/haldrenn.md>), Morlaith is remembered as the Briarheart, the fey power behind old bargains and dangerous crossings. Local stories say he helps keep worse things beyond the passages, but they also warn that a broken rule can bring ruin on a village. Children know the Hollow Man rhyme, associated with the minions the forest sends against those who take from it:

> Knock, knock, knock;  
> The Hollow Man calls.  
> Swing, swing, swing;  
> His greedy sickle falls.  
> Chop, chop, chop;  
> He takes another head.  
> Burn, burn, burn;  
> The Hollow Man is dead!
