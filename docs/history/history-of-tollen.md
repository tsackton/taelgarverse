# History of Tollen



This is a chronicle of the history of the [Free City of Tollen](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/tollen.md>), from its founding to the present day. Tollenders are deeply proud of their long history, and many will claim that Tollen is the oldest continually occupied [human](<../creatures/species/humans.md>) city in the world. Whether true or not is hard to know, but regardless Tollen's history stretches deep into the past.

## Founding Era
*before the Downfall*

Tollen was originally settled on the north bank of the [Volta](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/volta.md>) long ago, when the elves still ruled vast forests far to the south, and great lizardfolk swamps occupied the land that is now [Sembara](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/sembara/sembara.md>). The [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) say that the first Tollenders were their distant ancestors, a mariner people who came from [Hkar](<historical-realms/hkar.md>) on great ships and slowly explored and settled the shores of the [Green Sea](<../gazetteer/green-sea.md>), but it is clear that even from the beginning Tollen was a place where many cultures met and intermingled. This era lasted until the Downfall and the sinking of [Hkar](<historical-realms/hkar.md>) upended the world, reaching even as far away as the [Green Sea](<../gazetteer/green-sea.md>). 



## The Skaer Empire
*Downfall - DR 402*

In the aftermath of the Downfall, the seas shifted, the ocean turned violent, and the land dried up, the great swamps of the lizardfolk south of Tollen shrinking to small remnants. Many people die in storms and famine. During this time, the [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) emerge from the ancient seafarers who originally settled Tollen, and become the dominant people of the [Western Green Sea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/western-green-sea.md>), worshipping the ocean goddess [Kaikkea](<../gods-and-religions/gods/incorporeal-gods/kaikkea.md>). Tollen becomes a major port and trade center under [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) rule. However, to the south, the influence of the [Drankorian Empire](<historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>) is growing, and this era ends when the [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) sign the [Treaty of Marhavn](<../events/400s/402/treaty-of-marhavn.md>) with [Drankor](<historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>). 

Most people believe that the oldest parts of Tollen -- [Fiskurth](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/fiskurth.md>), [Skepwalk](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/skepwalk.md>), and [Aesganstrad](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/aesganstrad.md>) -- were settled during this era. 

- 402 DR: The [Treaty of Marhavn](<../events/400s/402/treaty-of-marhavn.md>) is signed between Emperor Goristo and the [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>), marking the end of the Skaer Empire in Tollen and the beginning of the Age of Drankor



## Age of Drankor
_DR 402 - DR 1059_

The [Treaty of Marhavn](<../events/400s/402/treaty-of-marhavn.md>) establishes the [Volta](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/volta.md>) as the northern border of the [Drankorian Empire](<historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>). Though [Tollen](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/tollen.md>), on the north bank, is not under Drankorian governence, [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) warships are forbidden to sail the [Volta](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/volta.md>), leaving [Tollen](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/tollen.md>) deeply reliant on the [Drankorian Empire](<historical-realms/drankorian-empire.md>) for protection. In practice, it becomes a Drankorian protectorate during the early part of the Age of Drankor. Throughout this period, though, Tollen retains its place as a cultural mixing ground, where many languages can be heard, and temples to the [Eight Divines](<../gods-and-religions/religions/mos-numena/mos-numena.md>) coexist with those dedicated to [Kaikkea](<../gods-and-religions/gods/incorporeal-gods/kaikkea.md>).   The [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>), though they diminish in power and influence, retain control over the [Temple of Kaikkea](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/places/temple-of-kaikkea.md>) and continue to maintain an important presence in the city. 

This is a time of significant expansion for the city. The [Tollen Bridge](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/places/tollen-bridge.md>) is constructed during this era by the Drankorians, as are the first stone walls of the city. [Nordgate](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/nordgate.md>), established around the original north gate of the city; [Bridgeward](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/bridgeward.md>) and [Southbridge](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/southbridge.md>), on the north and south sides of the Tollen Bridge; and [Magus Street](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/magus-street.md>), east of the older Skaer districts and away from the river, all date to this era. Bridgeward was a major commercial district during this era, and retains some of that character today, while Southbridge and Nordgate retain little of their ancient character. 

The Age of Drankor in Tollen ends when Drankor falls to the [First Plague](<../events/1000s/1059/first-plague.md>) in DR 1059. 

- 1059 DR: The [Fall of Drankor](<../events/1000s/1059/fall-of-drankor.md>) and the First Plague ends the Age of Drankor in Tollen



## The Free City
_DR 1059 - DR 1695_

Tollen first came to prominence as a small part of the Skaer empire, and grew as a city under the shadow of the Drankorian Empire. After the [Fall of Drankor](<../events/1000s/1059/fall-of-drankor.md>), it truly became a free city. Though little is recorded from the years immediately after the [Fall of Drankor](<../events/1000s/1059/fall-of-drankor.md>), by the time modern Sembara is established under [Derik I](<../people/historical-figures/sembaran-royalty/derik-i.md>), Tollen is a significant regional power, and during the [Great War](<../events/1500s/great-war.md>), Tollen is an ally to [Derik III](<../people/historical-figures/sembaran-royalty/derik-iii.md>). After the Great War, with the decline of [Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/cymea.md>) as a maritime power, Tollen's navy grows in strength and the city begins to project strength throughout the [Western Gulf](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/western-gulf.md>) and the [Straits of Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/straits-of-cymea.md>). 

During this long history, Tollen expanded in several waves. As the city grows, the walls are expanded to the north and west, incorporating [Godshome](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/godshome.md>) and [Guildgate](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/guildgate.md>) into the city. In the years before the Great War, the walls are expanded again, and [Fat Brook](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/fat-brook.md>) is covered; the western part of [Guildgate](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/guildgate.md>) (formerly Guildgate Outer) is enclosed in the walls at this point, as are the districts of [Fairgate Inner](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/fairgate-inner.md>) (then simply Fairgate) and [Haurhill](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/haurhill.md>), encompassing the hill where an old Drankorian fort once stood on the height of land above the [Little River](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/volta-watershed/little-river.md>). In this period, sometime near the time of the Great War, the [Battery](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/tollen/wards/battery.md>) ward, on the south bank of the Volta, is constructed to strengthen the defense the city. 

The end of this period is dated to the beginning of Tollish attempts to control Western Cymea.  

- 1695 DR: Tollen starts to establish colonies in [Western Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/western-cymea.md>), at least in part in response to pirate activity in the [Western Gulf](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/western-gulf.md>)



## Recent Events
_DR 1695 - current_

In recent years, Tollen has grown from an important city to the strongest naval power in the Western Gulf. The city has grown larger, with the wards of Fenslane and Tideswell, on the south bank of the Volta, becoming established in recent years. Tollen's influence has also grown, as now many settlements in [Western Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/western-cymea.md>) are functionally Tollish colonies, and Tollen's navies control the [Straits of Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/straits-of-cymea.md>).
