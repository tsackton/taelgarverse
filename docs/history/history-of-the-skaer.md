# History of the Skaer



This is a chronicle of the history of the [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>), from when they first settled on the shores of the [Green Sea](<../gazetteer/green-sea.md>) to the current day. 

## Origins
_before the Downfall_

The Skaer have a long history on the Green Sea. Their myths and legends speak of coming from the east on great ships; they are descended from the ancient seafaring culture that left [Hkar](<historical-realms/hkar.md>) and settled the [Green Sea](<../gazetteer/green-sea.md>) in the centuries before the [Downfall](<../events/ancient/the-downfall.md>). 



## The Skaer Empire
_Downfall to DR 402_

In the aftermath of the end of the Downfall Wars, the [Western Green Sea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/western-green-sea.md>) was left changed: the lizardfolk and elven empires that once dominated were gone, as the great swamps dried out.  During this period, the influence of the Skaer grew, as they established a significant maritime empire in the Western Green Sea. This era ended as the Skaer empire fell away before Drankorian expansion, sealing with the [Treaty of Marhavn](<../events/400s/402/treaty-of-marhavn.md>) in DR 402.

- 402 DR: The [Treaty of Marhavn](<../events/400s/402/treaty-of-marhavn.md>) between the [Skaer](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/skaer.md>) and the [Drankorian Empire](<drankorian-empire/drankorian-empire.md>) marks the end of Skaer dominance in the [Western Green Sea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/western-green-sea.md>). 




## Retrenchment
_DR 402 - DR 1059_

As the Drankorian Empire establishes dominance over the [Western Green Sea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/western-green-sea.md>), and especially the [Western Gulf](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/western-gulf.md>), the Skaer retreat north, remaining in coastal [Vostok](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/vostok/vostok.md>) and especially the [Skaer Islands](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/skaerhem/skaerhem.md>), which are called [Skaegenland](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/skaerhem/skaerhem.md>) by the Drankorians and later peoples of [Greater Sembara](<../gazetteer/greater-sembara/greater-sembara.md>). 



## Competition
_DR 1059 - DR 1700ish

After the fall of Drankor, the Skaer culture grows in the absence of Drankorian control, does not establish an empire or control significant territory within the Western Gulf, partly due to increased maritime competition from [Cymea](<../gazetteer/western-green-sea/realms/cymea.md>) in this era.
