The Dominion of Avatus was a realm in the [Upper Istaros](<../../gazetteer/upper-istaros/upper-istaros.md>) and [Greater Sembara](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/greater-sembara.md>). Founded by its mysterious ruler [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>) on the [Aurbez Plateau](<../../gazetteer/upper-istaros/aurbez-plateau.md>) and lower foothills of the [Sentinels](<../../gazetteer/sentinel-range.md>), at its greatest extent it spread east along the [Andonne](<../../gazetteer/major-rivers/istaros-watershed/andonne.md>) to the edges of the [Darkwood](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/darkwood.md>), and north to the [Enst](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/wistel-enst-watershed/enst.md>). Briefly, much of the [Duchy of Wisford](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/sembara/heartlands/duchy-of-wisford.md>) and the Duchy of Brovna was occupied by the Dominion, until [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>) was defeated by [Derik I](<../../people/historical-figures/sembaran-royalty/derik-i.md>) and his realm collapsed. 

## History
_See more: [Serpentine Wars](<../../events/1300s/serpentine-wars.md>)_



Although the exact origins of the Dominion are unclear, by the middle of the DR 1200s, the realm was established in the [Aurbez Plateau](<../../gazetteer/upper-istaros/aurbez-plateau.md>), the [Aursen](<../../gazetteer/major-rivers/istaros-watershed/aursen.md>), and parts of the [Andonne Plain](<../../gazetteer/upper-istaros/andonne-plain.md>). For many years, a tense peace seemed to have existed among the Dominion, the [Istabor Alliance](<istabor-alliance.md>) and [Isingue](<../../gazetteer/upper-istaros/isingue.md>) to the south, and the various minor lords and the [Duchy of Maseau](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/duchy-of-maseau/duchy-of-maseau.md>) to the north. 

This tentative, watchful peace ended decisively in the DR 1350s. Over the course of the next fifty years or so, a series of wars broke out between the Dominion and its northern neighbors, including the [Duchy of Maseau](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/duchy-of-maseau/duchy-of-maseau.md>) and [Addermarch](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/addermarch.md>). These wars, later known as the [Serpentine Wars](<../../events/1300s/serpentine-wars.md>), primarily arose from [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>) seeking to control new sources of [serpentine](<../../things/materials/serpentine.md>). Over the course of fifty years, the Dominion of Avatus subjugated the [Duchy of Maseau](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/duchy-of-maseau/duchy-of-maseau.md>), and brutally occupied, but then lost, much of western [Addermarch](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/addermarch/addermarch.md>). 

While an uneasy peace holds for a few decades in the early DR 1400s, a series of conflicts in the early DR 1420s lead to [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>) occupying significant parts of the [Enst River](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/rivers/wistel-enst-watershed/enst.md>) valley. Soon after, [Derik I](<../../people/historical-figures/sembaran-royalty/derik-i.md>) unifies [Sembara](<../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/sembara/sembara.md>) under the [House of Sewick](<../../groups/sembaran-noble-houses/house-of-sewick.md>) and defeats [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>), destroying the [Dominion of Avatus](<dominion-of-avatus.md>) in its entirety.
## Culture and Politics
Little is known or remembered about the culture and politics of the Dominion, and what is recorded was, in large part, written by [Derik I](<../../people/historical-figures/sembaran-royalty/derik-i.md>) after his defeat of [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>). Most notable were the armies of the Dominion. Avatus recruited numerous hobgoblins to his banner, and his armies had a reputation for tactical brilliance and viciousness in battle, enhanced by their use of mind-altering [Serpentine](<../../things/materials/serpentine.md>) weapons that drove their bears into unstoppable frenzies. Hobgoblin troops, aided at times by werewolfs and other monsters, made up the bulk of his armies, but this was not a hobgoblin kingdom and humans and occasionally others did serve in the army. Whether this was out of true loyalty, desperation, coercion, or something else is lost to time. 

The Dominion was a deeply closed society: little news traveled in, or out, though at times trade and commerce did pass through parts of the Dominion. The ruler, [Avatus](<../../people/historical-figures/avatus.md>), styled himself the Emperor of New Drankor, and the Dominion did call back to Drankorian styles of monumental architecture at times. Rumors and stories of those who survived under the Dominion's rule suggest a culture of restricted movement, hard work, and strict obedience, where hobgoblins and others who were willing to serve with them in the armies of the Dominion enjoyed great privilege.
