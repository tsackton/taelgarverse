## Narrative

The slide ends with the party in the black-stone chamber where [Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) has feared the shaggy white beast. The creature calls up Living Snow echoes that punish repeated tactics and redirect attacks away from it, forcing the party to probe for weaknesses while keeping [Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) out of sight.

Ekko's conjured pack, Justas's Spirit Guardians, Finnan's fey spirit, [Rala](<../../../people/pcs/into-the-chasm/rala.md>), and Eolo's psionic strikes turn the fight into a pattern-breaking contest. When the final echoes fall, the exposed beast dies under Eolo's psionic volley and the chamber begins to change around the party.

As the beast dies, the unnatural chamber softens into earth and granite and exposes a way out of [Zeyfa's Labyrinth](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/zimkova/zeyfa-s-labyrinth.md>). The party pauses long enough to take the creature's head as a trophy and to read the changed space for meaning before trusting Kalima's route forward.

The earthen tunnel carries them out onto a cliffside view of Melusa, the village [Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) has been trying to reach. From there, the party also sends a fey spirit toward the Ice Palace in search of Forquarion the Frostbinder, tying their escape from the maze to the power still waiting above the village.

[Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) hurries the party up switchbacks and into Melusa, a cliffside village shaped by fog, water, floating rocks, and the open drop of the [Great Chasm](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/zimkova/great-chasm.md>). The party sees five gashes below a massive ice wolf statue, marking the village as stranger and more dangerous than a simple refuge.

At Nura and Khaled's house, Kalima's family receives the party with caution and urgency while Samir sleeps nearby. [Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) uses Justas's light-enchanted stone as proof that the [Kestavo](<../../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/kestavo.md>) are more than stories, turning the party's arrival into a challenge to what her family thought was possible.

The conversation in Nura and Khaled's house shifts from refuge to explanation. Melusa survives in fog and repetition, with its people taught to endure rather than expect rescue, and with [Kestavo](<../../../gods-and-religions/religions/northern-folk-religions/kestavo.md>) reduced for many to half-remembered tales.

Kalima's family explains how sunset, sacrifice, children, death, and memory shape the village's rules. The Ice Palace gong governs when the village sleeps, and the party learns that even being remembered can matter when souls are trapped inside Melusa's curse.

After the village settles under the gong, [Ekko](<../../../people/pcs/into-the-chasm/ekko.md>) slips out as a gray cat to scout the gashes and the strange openings around Melusa. The mirror-like gash has human guards, watchful wisps, and an ice wolf statue, while the other openings suggest hostile realms beyond the cliff.

[Finnan](<../../../people/pcs/into-the-chasm/finnan-oakstride.md>) extends the search with Clairvoyance and sees a circular disk in the Ice Palace covered in dense [Sylvan writing](<../../../background/sylvan-writing.md>) and pale light. Below it, soldiers prepare to hunt the trickster spirit the party sent ahead, and an ice bridge grows outward toward Melusa while the party hides and tries to rest.

## Combat

- Living Snow in the Black-Stone Chamber: Shaggy white beast, Living Snow echoes. The party keeps [Kalima](<../../../people/sembarans/kalima.md>) hidden, breaks the Living Snow echoes protecting the shaggy white beast, kills the beast, and opens an earthen route out of [Zeyfa's Labyrinth](<../../../gazetteer/greater-sembara/zimkova/zeyfa-s-labyrinth.md>).
